## II. THE PRIOR ART (CORE TECHNOLOGY)
The Anslow Process solves the catastrophic carburization and embrittlement caused when sCO2 acts as a hyper-solvent against traditional metallic containment vessels at extreme parameters (e.g., 700°C, 30 MPa). The technology comprises the following interdependent innovations:

1. **The Vitreous-Carbon FGM Architecture:** A material transitioning seamlessly from a chemically inert Silicon Carbide (SiC) inner face to a high-tensile Carbon-Fiber Reinforced Carbon (C/C) outer shell, utilizing a precise 4.2mm transition zone to eliminate boundary layer spallation.
2. **Continuous Gradient Extrusion:** A manufacturing method utilizing a circular extrusion die with concentric micro-injectors and a highly viscous Paraffin Wax binder to create a stable "Green State" pipe without homogenizing pre-ceramic polymers.
3. **Supercritical Fluid Extraction (SFE) Debinding:** Utilizing sCO2 as a zero-surface-tension hyper-solvent to non-destructively extract the wax without capillary action.
4. **Microwave-Assisted Hot Isostatic Pressing (HIP):** Simultaneous 30,000 PSI Argon pressure and microwave heat to achieve a continuous atomic curve and zero porosity.
5. **The Anslow Cogeneration Loop:** A closed-loop factory utilizing waste Argon kinetic blow-off via a Free-Piston Compander to crush sCO2, and Recuperating Heat Exchangers to achieve near-zero parasitic electrical load.
