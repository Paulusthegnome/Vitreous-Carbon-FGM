## 🔬 ABSTRACT
The transition to supercritical Carbon Dioxide (sCO2) Brayton cycles represents a paradigm shift in thermal power generation, offering unprecedented thermodynamic efficiencies. However, sCO2 acts as a hyper-solvent at operational parameters (e.g., 700°C, 30 MPa), causing severe carburization and embrittlement in traditional metallic containment vessels. 

This project open-sources a novel material architecture: a Vitreous-Carbon Functionally Graded Material (FGM), transitioning seamlessly from a chemically inert Silicon Carbide (SiC) inner face to a high-tensile Carbon-Fiber Reinforced Carbon (C/C) outer shell. 

Crucially, this repository details the "Anslow Process," a revolutionary manufacturing methodology that solves the historical bottlenecks of FGM fabrication. By utilizing continuous gradient extrusion, a transient paraffin-wax binder, Supercritical Fluid Extraction (SFE) for non-destructive debinding, and Microwave-Assisted Hot Isostatic Pressing (HIP) for zero-porosity sintering, we present a scalable, economically viable pathway to unbreakable sCO2 containment.

## ⚙️ CORE INNOVATIONS

**1. The 4.2mm Golden Ratio**
A simple lamination or coating of SiC onto a Carbon substrate fails due to the Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) mismatch, leading to spallation. The Anslow Process utilizes an exact 4.2mm transition zone from 100% SiC to 100% C/C, allowing the material to expand and contract as a single, unified organism. 

**2. Continuous Gradient Extrusion & SFE Debinding**
Bypassing the exorbitant time costs of Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and the "Recoater Problem" of 3D printing, the process extrudes a "Green State" pipe utilizing a specialized Paraffin Wax binder. The pipe is then flooded with sCO2, which acts as a hyper-solvent to non-destructively extract the wax without the capillary action that destroys powder gradients during thermal debinding.

**3. Microwave-Assisted Hot Isostatic Pressing (HIP)**
The debound, porous pipe is pressurized in a microwave furnace with inert Argon gas up to 30,000 PSI. This extreme heat and omnidirectional pressure squeeze micro-pores out of existence and cause the mechanically stepped gradient to melt and diffuse into a mathematically perfect, continuous atomic curve.

**4. Absolute Industrial Ecology (The Cogeneration Loop)**
The factory operates as a closed-loop thermodynamic recursion. A Free-Piston Compander utilizes the 30,000 PSI kinetic force of the Argon blow-off to kinetically crush the sCO2 to 4,300 PSI, bypassing the electrical compressor entirely and reducing the parasitic load of the turbine to near zero.
